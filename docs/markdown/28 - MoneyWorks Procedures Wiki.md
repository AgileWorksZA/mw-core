MoneyWorks Procedures Wiki

Although MoneyWorks as a whole is documented extensively in this manual, the
routines and procedures for how your business utilise MoneyWorks are not. The
MoneyWorks Procedures Wiki, available in Gold/Datacentre, provides a central
repository of documentation about your business and operations.

You are probably already familiar with the term wikifrom wikipedia:

A wiki is a form of hypertext publication on the internet which is
collaboratively edited and managed by its audience directly through a web
browser. A typical wiki contains multiple pages that can either be edited by
the public or limited to use within an organization for maintaining its
internal knowledge base.

The MoneyWorks Procedures Wiki allows authorised users to view and edit your
organisation's knowledge base. Authorised users edit existing pages or create
new pages. Each page can be formatted with text, lists, colours and images, and
can link to other pages in the Wiki (or to websites).

The wiki is only accessible from within MoneyWorks and cannot be accessed
through a browser (the Print button will actually display the current page in your
default browser so you can print it or keep it open outside of MoneyWorks, but
the links are disabled and you will not be able to edit it).

Accessing the Wiki

File administrators can view and modify the Wiki. For other users, access is
determined by the View Wikiand Modify Wikiprivileges. These must be set by
your administrator before you can access the wiki.

When the page is displayed in your browser, you can use the browser's print
function to print the page. Note that the links to other Wiki pages will not work
in your browser (but link's to external sites will).

Notes on searching:

• Searching is not case sensitive.
• The search is a word search only. You cannot search for punctuation or

special characters.

• Multiple words act as an "AND" search. For example searching for payables
overdue will find all pages with the the words payablesand overdue(they
need not be adjacent). This is equivalent to searching for payables AND
overdue.

• To find adjacent words, use +, or enclose the search in double quotes. For
example overdue + payables will match overdue payables, but not
payables that are overdue. This is equivalent to searching for "overdue
payables".

• Append an asterisk to a word to act as a wild card. For example searching

for pay* will find all pages with words starting with pay(e.g. pay, payment,
payable, payables).

• Use NOT to find pages with the first term and not the second. For example
payable* NOT receivable* would find pages with words starting with
payablethat do not also have words starting with receivable.

• Use OR to find pages with either term, e.g. payable* OR receivable*.
• Note that AND, NOT and OR must be in uppercase in your search.

Printing a Wiki Page

A Wiki is not like a book or other linear document, and hence cannot be printed.
You can however print individual pages by clicking the Printbutton. When you
do this, MoneyWorks will try to display the page in your default browser, which
is better at printing web pages than MoneyWorks (an extremely rare occurence
of a browser being better than MoneyWorks!).
