# MoneyWorks Lead Qualification Criteria Framework

## Qualification Scoring System

Use this framework to score prospects from 1-10 in each category. Total scores determine qualification level:
- **35-40**: Highly Qualified (immediate sales engagement)
- **25-34**: Qualified (demonstration and proposal phase)
- **15-24**: Nurturing Required (educational content and follow-up)
- **Below 15**: Disqualified (polite disengagement)

## 1. Business Fit (10 points maximum)

### High Fit (8-10 points)
**Target Industries:**
- Manufacturing/Distribution (complex inventory)
- Professional Services (project-based billing)
- Multi-location retail/hospitality
- Construction/Engineering (job costing)
- Healthcare/Education (departmental accounting)

**Business Characteristics:**
- 20-500 employees
- $2M-$50M annual revenue
- Multiple locations or departments
- Complex reporting requirements
- Project or job-based operations

### Medium Fit (5-7 points)
- Single location but growing rapidly
- Service-based business with time tracking needs
- Retail with moderate inventory complexity
- Any business outgrowing QuickBooks/Xero

### Low Fit (1-4 points)
- Very small business (under 10 employees)
- Simple cash-based business
- No growth plans or ambitions
- Industry with specialized software requirements

### No Fit (0 points)
- Pure cash business (restaurants, bars)
- Government entities with specific requirements
- Businesses requiring industry-specific compliance (banking, insurance)

## 2. Current Pain Level (10 points maximum)

### High Pain (8-10 points)
**Critical Indicators:**
- "We're drowning in manual processes"
- "Month-end takes us 2+ weeks"
- "We can't get real-time financial information"
- "Our auditors are concerned about our controls"
- "We're managing multiple QuickBooks files"
- "We can't track profitability by project/location"
- "Our current system crashes frequently"
- "We've hit user or transaction limits"

### Medium Pain (5-7 points)
- "Things work but could be better"
- "We spend too much time on data entry"
- "Reporting is limited and time-consuming"
- "We're growing and worried about scalability"
- "Integration between systems is challenging"

### Low Pain (1-4 points)
- "Everything is mostly fine"
- "We're managing okay with current system"
- "Haven't really thought about changing"
- "Too busy to worry about accounting software"

### No Pain (0 points)
- "We're completely satisfied"
- "Just implemented new system recently"
- "Everything is automated and working perfectly"

## 3. Growth Trajectory (10 points maximum)

### High Growth (8-10 points)
**Strong Indicators:**
- 20%+ annual revenue growth
- Adding new locations within 12 months
- Hiring 5+ new employees this year
- Launching new product lines or services
- Planning acquisition or merger
- Expanding to new markets/geographies
- Seeking external funding or investment

### Moderate Growth (5-7 points)
- 10-20% annual growth
- Stable business with expansion plans
- Adding new departments or divisions
- Considering new locations in 2+ years
- Gradual staff increases

### Slow Growth (1-4 points)
- 0-10% annual growth
- Maintaining current operations
- No specific expansion plans
- Conservative business approach

### Declining/Stagnant (0 points)
- Flat or declining revenue
- Downsizing operations
- Survival mode business
- No investment in improvements

## 4. Budget & Authority (10 points maximum)

### High Authority/Budget (8-10 points)
**Decision Maker Indicators:**
- CEO, CFO, or Business Owner
- "I make these decisions"
- Has allocated budget for business improvements
- Previous software purchase experience
- Understands ROI of business systems
- Can approve $10K+ purchases
- Willing to discuss specific budget ranges

### Medium Authority/Budget (5-7 points)
- Controller, Finance Manager, or Operations Manager
- "I evaluate and recommend"
- Budget approval process exists
- Some decision-making influence
- Needs to build business case internally
- Price-conscious but not price-only focused

### Low Authority/Budget (1-4 points)
- Junior staff or end-user
- "I need to check with my boss"
- No budget allocated currently
- Price is primary concern
- Limited decision-making influence

### No Authority/Budget (0 points)
- Pure information gatherer
- "Just looking at options"
- No budget and no approval process
- Cannot influence decisions
- Student or competitor research

## Additional Qualification Factors

### Timing Indicators (Bonus/Penalty Points)

**Add 2 points for:**
- Need solution implemented this quarter
- Current system contract expiring
- Upcoming audit or compliance deadline
- New fiscal year planning
- Major business milestone approaching

**Subtract 2 points for:**
- "Just starting to look"
- No specific timeline
- "Maybe next year"
- Recently implemented current system
- Major business disruption anticipated

### Competitive Situation (Bonus/Penalty Points)

**Add 1 point for:**
- Currently using QuickBooks or Xero
- Multiple systems creating inefficiencies
- No relationship with current vendor
- Open to changing systems

**Subtract 1 point for:**
- Recently purchased competitive system
- Long-term contract with competitor
- Strong relationship with current vendor
- Multiple quotes already received

### Technical Readiness (Bonus/Penalty Points)

**Add 1 point for:**
- Internal IT resources available
- Previous successful implementations
- Cloud-ready infrastructure
- Integration requirements understood

**Subtract 1 point for:**
- Resistance to technology changes
- No technical resources
- Complex legacy system constraints
- Unrealistic technical expectations

## Qualification Process Workflow

### Initial Screening (First 5 minutes)
1. **Business Fit Assessment**
   - Industry and business model
   - Company size and complexity
   - Geographic distribution

2. **Pain Point Discovery**
   - Current system frustrations
   - Manual process pain
   - Growth-related challenges

### Detailed Qualification (10-15 minutes)
3. **Growth and Timeline Assessment**
   - Expansion plans and timeline
   - Business growth rate
   - Implementation urgency

4. **Budget and Authority Discovery**
   - Decision-making process
   - Budget allocation approach
   - Purchase authority level

### Scoring and Next Steps
- **Calculate total score**
- **Identify qualification level**
- **Determine appropriate next action**
- **Set follow-up timeline**

## Disqualification Criteria (Immediate)

### Hard Disqualifiers
- Outside target business size (under 10 or over 1000 employees)
- Industry requiring specialized software (banking, insurance, healthcare with specific compliance)
- Recently implemented competitive solution (within 12 months)
- No decision-making authority and cannot connect to decision maker
- Unrealistic budget expectations (expecting enterprise features for under $1000)
- Hostile or uncooperative during qualification call

### Soft Disqualifiers (Consider for nurturing)
- Good fit but no current budget
- Right business but wrong timing
- Interested but needs education about solutions
- Influencer without authority but willing to champion

## Documentation Requirements

For each qualified lead, document:
- **Business Fit Score** and reasoning
- **Pain Level Score** with specific examples
- **Growth Score** with supporting evidence
- **Budget/Authority Score** with decision process details
- **Key stakeholders** identified
- **Specific pain points** mentioned
- **Timeline requirements** discussed
- **Budget range** (if disclosed)
- **Next step commitments** made
- **Follow-up schedule** agreed

## Quality Assurance

### Review qualified leads for:
- **Completeness** of qualification information
- **Accuracy** of scoring based on conversation
- **Realistic** next step commitments
- **Proper** handoff information for sales team
- **Clear** disqualification reasoning for rejected leads

This framework ensures consistent, thorough qualification while maintaining focus on high-value prospects who are most likely to benefit from MoneyWorks solutions.
